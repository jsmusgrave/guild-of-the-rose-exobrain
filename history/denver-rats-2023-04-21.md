# What I Changed for this talk
I made some additions to our format that I think workout out well.  I asked everyone to wear a double nametag including both their name and their discord handle.  This helped me–as a bit of a noob–to be more comfortable not knowing everyone's name and I think it makes the meetups more approachable to new people.

Timing-wise we scheduled more time for the venue (4 hours)
* Setup (1/2 an hour before start time),
* Wait for people to meander in (1/2 an hour after start time)
* Run the workshop (90 min)
* Socialize (about 30-60 min)
* Cleanup (1/2 an hour)

# Initial Thoughts

* Folks didn't really get the "Mental Sweep" or the "Areas of responsibility" or indeed have a mental list of projects and roles in their life.  As such, it was hard to figure out how to recomend someone engage with tooling meant to achieve goals when the goals weren't clear.  It's my belief that your systems and tools should flow out of your roles and goals, so it was discouraging to me that I couldn't get engagement on this.

* I was really expecting attendees to be more developer-y as a cohort and as such have more familiarity with some of the concepts.  I didn't expect to have to explain Kanaban, for example.  I would spend more time helping people understanding the different approaches of tools and systems in more depth.  I doubt they left with appreciating when a Kanban board would be a good tool and when something like Omnifocus would be a good tool.  

* I would provide a broad list of archetypes of people using these systems.  The point of this would be to allow attendees to see themselves and their problems, projects and aspirations in those archetypes and help them align with tools and systems.

* I would have more detailed and structured content around Roles, Areas and Horizons to really get folks into it.  This does have a high likelyhood of turning some folks off.  But, I think this sort of clarity is super important to these efforts.

* Folks really didn't want to participate in trying the tools out.  I suspect that reflects a couple factors.  First was poor direction and content from me in how to participate as general.  What was intuitive to me was not intuitive to the attendees.  Second, I think there was a general disposition on the part of the attendee population not to be introspective in this way without a lot of motivation and structure.  And lastly, it needed to feel more fun and salient.

*  I would propose breaking this up into multiple sessions.  
    * Understanding your goals, roles, and horizons.
    * Second would be understanding what systems would be helpful in achieving those goals.
